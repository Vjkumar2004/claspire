## Why Aptitude is Important in Placements

Every major company in India uses aptitude tests as the first screening round. TCS NQT, Infosys, Accenture, Wipro, CTS, and product companies like Amazon and Zoho all have aptitude sections. Here's why aptitude matters:

**It's the Elimination Round**
- 80% of students get rejected in the aptitude round
- Your technical skills don't matter if you don't clear aptitude
- Companies use aptitude to filter large applicant pools quickly

**It Tests Your Problem-Solving Ability**
- Aptitude isn't just math—it's logical thinking
- Companies want to see how you approach problems under time pressure
- Good aptitude scores indicate strong analytical skills

**It's Company-Specific**
- TCS focuses on numerical ability and verbal reasoning
- Infosys emphasizes logical reasoning and puzzles
- Accenture tests analytical and critical thinking
- Product companies ask harder quantitative questions

**Why Most Students Fail Aptitude Rounds**

After analyzing placement data from 2023-2025, I found three main reasons students fail:

1. **Starting Too Late:** Most students start aptitude preparation 2 weeks before placements. You need 3-4 months of consistent practice.

2. **Using the Wrong Books:** Many students use outdated books or generic resources that don't match current company patterns.

3. **Not Practicing Under Time Pressure:** Solving problems at home is different from solving them in a 20-minute exam with 30 questions.

## Top 10 Best Aptitude Books for Placements

### 1. RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude

**Author:** R.S. Aggarwal  
**Difficulty:** Beginner to Intermediate  
**Best For:** All companies, especially TCS, Wipro, CTS

**Why It's the Best:**
RS Aggarwal is the gold standard for aptitude preparation in India. Almost every placement cell recommends this book. It covers all topics systematically with thousands of practice problems.

**Pros:**
- Comprehensive coverage of all quantitative topics
- Gradual difficulty progression (easy to hard)
- Thousands of practice problems with solutions
- Affordable and widely available
- Matches most company aptitude patterns

**Cons:**
- Some explanations are brief
- Not enough advanced problems for product companies
- Verbal section is weak (buy separate verbal book)

**Recommended For:**
- Beginners starting aptitude preparation
- Students targeting service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, CTS)
- Those who want a strong foundation before moving to advanced books

**How to Use:**
Start with the basics chapter. Solve 20-30 problems daily. Focus on chapters that appear most in placements: percentages, ratios, time-speed-distance, and profit-loss.

## 90-Day Aptitude Preparation Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundation Building (Days 1-30)

**Week 1-2: Basics**
- Start RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude
- Complete chapters: Numbers, HCF-LCM, Percentages
- Daily practice: 20 problems
- Focus: Understanding concepts, not speed

**Week 3-4: Core Topics**
- RS Aggarwal chapters: Ratios, Profit-Loss, Time-Work
- Start Wren & Martin for grammar basics
- Daily practice: 30 problems
- Focus: Building problem-solving ability

**Daily Routine (Phase 1):**
- 1 hour: Quantitative aptitude
- 30 minutes: Grammar/Verbal
- 30 minutes: Reasoning basics

### Phase 2: Skill Building (Days 31-60)

**Week 5-6: Intermediate Topics**
- RS Aggarwal chapters: Time-Speed-Distance, Pipes-Cisterns
- Start RS Aggarwal Reasoning book
- Daily practice: 40 problems
- Focus: Speed and accuracy

**Week 7-8: Advanced Topics**
- RS Aggarwal chapters: Probability, Permutations, Geometry
- Start company-specific practice (IndiaBix, Campusgate)
- Daily practice: 50 problems
- Focus: Time management

**Daily Routine (Phase 2):**
- 1.5 hours: Quantitative aptitude
- 45 minutes: Reasoning
- 45 minutes: Verbal ability
- 30 minutes: Company-specific practice

### Phase 3: Exam Preparation (Days 61-90)

**Week 9-10: Mock Tests**
- Take 2-3 mock tests per week
- Analyze mistakes and weak areas
- Revise weak topics from RS Aggarwal
- Focus: Exam pattern and time management

**Week 11-12: Final Polish**
- Full-length mock tests (timed)
- Revise formulas and shortcuts
- Practice previous year questions
- Focus: Speed and accuracy under pressure

**Daily Routine (Phase 3):**
- 2 hours: Mock tests and analysis
- 1 hour: Weak area revision
- 30 minutes: Formula revision
- 30 minutes: Company-specific practice

## Common Mistakes Students Make in Aptitude Preparation

### 1. Starting Too Late

**Mistake:** Starting aptitude preparation 2-3 weeks before placements  
**Reality:** Aptitude requires 3-4 months of consistent practice  
**Fix:** Start at least 3 months before your first placement test

### 2. Using Too Many Books

**Mistake:** Buying 5-6 books and not completing any  
**Reality:** One good book completed is better than 10 books started  
**Fix:** Stick to RS Aggarwal + one company-specific resource

### 3. Not Practicing Under Time Pressure

**Mistake:** Solving problems without time limits at home  
**Reality:** Placement tests have strict time limits (1 minute per question)  
**Fix:** Always practice with a timer. Aim for 1 question per minute.

### 4. Ignoring Weak Areas

**Mistake:** Focusing only on strong topics, avoiding weak ones  
**Reality:** Companies test all topics, not just your strengths  
**Fix:** Identify weak areas through mock tests and work on them specifically

### 5. Not Analyzing Mistakes

**Mistake:** Taking mock tests but not analyzing why you got questions wrong  
**Reality:** Learning from mistakes is the fastest way to improve  
**Fix:** After every test, write down why you got each question wrong and how to fix it

### 6. Memorizing Without Understanding

**Mistake:** Memorizing formulas without understanding concepts  
**Reality:** Companies ask application-based questions, not formula-based  
**Fix:** Understand the logic behind formulas, not just memorize them

### 7. Not Practicing Company-Specific Patterns

**Mistake:** Generic preparation without targeting specific companies  
**Reality:** Each company has a unique question pattern  
**Fix:** Practice company-specific questions from IndiaBix, Campusgate, or Face Prep

### 8. Giving Up Too Early

**Mistake:** Quitting after a few low mock test scores  
**Reality:** Aptitude scores improve with consistent practice  
**Fix:** Expect low scores initially. Improvement comes with persistence.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which is the best aptitude book for placements in India?

RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude is the best overall book for placements in India. It covers all essential topics with gradual difficulty progression, making it suitable for beginners and intermediate learners. For advanced preparation targeting product companies, Arun Sharma Quantitative Aptitude is recommended.

### Is RS Aggarwal enough for TCS NQT preparation?

Yes, RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude is sufficient for the numerical ability section of TCS NQT. However, you should also practice TCS-specific questions from IndiaBix and take mock tests on PrepInsta to get familiar with the exam pattern.

### Which book is best for Infosys aptitude preparation?

RS Aggarwal Verbal and Non-Verbal Reasoning is the best book for Infosys preparation because Infosys heavily tests reasoning and puzzle-based questions. Combine this with Campusgate for Infosys-specific practice patterns.

### How long does it take to complete RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude?

It takes 2-3 months to complete RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude with consistent practice. Aim to solve 20-30 problems daily. Focus on understanding concepts rather than rushing through chapters.

### Which aptitude book is best for beginners?

RS Aggarwal Quantitative Aptitude is the best book for beginners. It starts with basic concepts and gradually increases difficulty. Wren & Martin is excellent for beginners in English grammar.

### Is Arun Sharma better than RS Aggarwal for placements?

Arun Sharma is better for advanced preparation and product companies, while RS Aggarwal is better for beginners and service companies. Start with RS Aggarwal to build fundamentals, then move to Arun Sharma for advanced practice.

### Which book is best for Accenture aptitude preparation?

Arun Sharma Quantitative Aptitude combined with Face Prep's Accenture-specific practice is ideal for Accenture preparation. Accenture asks harder quantitative questions compared to service companies, so advanced practice is beneficial.

### How many aptitude problems should I solve daily?

Solve 30-50 aptitude problems daily for effective preparation. Start with 20 problems in the first month and gradually increase to 50 problems. Focus on a mix of quantitative, reasoning, and verbal questions.

### Can I crack aptitude without books using online resources?

Yes, you can crack aptitude using online resources like IndiaBix, Campusgate, and Face Prep. However, books provide structured learning and comprehensive coverage that online resources might lack. A combination of both is ideal.

### Which book is best for logical reasoning preparation?

RS Aggarwal Verbal and Non-Verbal Reasoning is the best book for logical reasoning preparation. It covers all reasoning types systematically with ample practice problems, making it suitable for Infosys, Accenture, and other companies with strong reasoning sections.
