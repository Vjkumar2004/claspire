Getting your first job in India is hard. Applying through portals like Naukri or LinkedIn often feels like throwing your resume into a black hole — hundreds of applicants, zero responses.

But students who get referrals from college seniors? They skip the queue entirely.

## Why College Senior Referrals Work in India

In India's tech hiring ecosystem, an internal referral from a current employee can move your resume from the general pool directly to the hiring manager's desk. Most Indian companies — TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Zoho, Freshworks, startups — have formal referral programs where employees get bonuses for successful hires.

Your college senior already knows your background, your college, your hustle. That makes them the most natural referral source you have.

## Step 1: Find Seniors from Your Own College Who Are Already Placed

This is where most students fail — they message random alumni on LinkedIn who have no connection to them. The conversion rate is near zero.

Instead, focus only on seniors from your own college. They share your background. They remember what it was like to be in your position. They are far more likely to help.

Platforms like Claspire are built specifically for this — connecting students with verified seniors from their own campus across India.

## Step 2: Don't Ask for a Referral in Your First Message

This is the biggest mistake. Your first message should never say "can you refer me?"

Instead, ask something specific and genuine:

- "I saw you work at Zoho — I have an interview next week, could you share what the process was like?"
- "I'm from [your college], you graduated in 2022 — I'm preparing for placements and would love 10 minutes of your time."

Build a small connection first. Referrals follow naturally from genuine conversations.

## Step 3: Make It Easy for Them to Say Yes

When you do ask for a referral, reduce the friction completely:

- Have your resume ready as a PDF link
- Tell them exactly which role and which location you're applying for
- Write a 3-line summary of why you're a good fit — so they can copy-paste it into their internal referral form

The easier you make it, the more likely they'll follow through.

## Step 4: Follow Up Once — Politely

Seniors are busy. One polite follow-up after 5-7 days is completely acceptable. After that, respect their time.
